**Dragons** are a sapient species within the [[Cosmere\|cosmere]]. Originally, they came from [[Yolen\|Yolen]], but can now be found on other worlds (typically in very small numbers). They are one of Yolen's three sapient species, alongside humans and [[Sho Del\|Sho Del]]. Like the Sho Del, Dragons are a form of [[Fain\|fainlife]]. Young dragons are called **Dragonets**.

## Physiology
  <<Hoid\|Hoid>> with <<Frost\|Frost>>
>“*She had another shape as well. Many of them, but one deeper and truer than the others.*”
\-Taravangian, on Koravellium Avast[9]


Most dragons in the cosmere are [[Fain\|fain]] creatures, and as such they are hexapedal, with four legs and two wings. They age extremely slowly after reaching maturity, living tens of thousands of years, but they are not unkillable. Dragons take over thirty years to reach maturity. Dragons have stronger eyesight and hearing than humans.
In their true forms, they are not necessarily large; some are smaller than [[Sandling\|sandlings]], while others can be as large as [[Chasmfiend\|chasmfiends]]. Their skin is covered with pearlescent scales, as well as silvery streaks of [[Dragonsteel (metal)\|dragonsteel]] that run along the body, mostly the limbs, up towards their horns, which are made entirely of dragonsteel. Even in this nonhuman form, they are still able to speak with a human-sounding voice. Despite being reptilian, they are able to grow hair across the head, and under the neck and chin as a beard. At least some of them are carnivorous.

### Shapeshifting
>“*You are always a dragon, whether or not you have the shape of one.*”
\-Frost to Starling.[8]

Dragons have the inherent ability to shapeshift. The scope of their shapeshifting powers may vary by individual, but shapeshifting is a core aspect of their life cycle. When in their humanoid form, dragons do not display any identifiable nonhuman characteristics. Their shapeshifting method is different to that of the [[Kandra\|kandra]], even if the result is similar.
Dragons can be locked into their human form with silver manacles made from dragons.

#### The Transformation
Dragonets are stuck in their human form until they become thirty years old. Then, on their exact birthday, they will absorb the first light of [[Yolen\|Yolen's]] dawn and transform into their draconic form. It is not entirely clear if they only transform at the first light of Yolen's dawn, or if they would transform on any planet.
Before First Light appears, it is customary for the parent to speak of the ritual importance of inhabiting a human body for thirty years. The Dragons consider it to be [[Adonalsium\|Adonalsium's]] wisdom, teaching them to understand other, much less grand, species.

#### Maturity
After the transformation, dragons live in their draconic form for decades to master their abilities. After that, they live their lives in both forms equally. They assume their humanoid form when mating and bearing children. They can also reproduce with humans, and the child will be a dragon.

## Abilities
  A dragon's head
Dragons can subsist directly on energy drawn from the [[Spiritual Realm\|Spiritual Realm]]. Due to this, dragons do not need to eat, drink, or sleep. They also have special organs that fabricate nutrients and water by metabolizing [[Investiture\|Investiture]] from the Spiritual Realm. This [[Connection\|Connection]] can be severed, but is extremely difficult to do. Dragons can choose to have their bodies sleep, and they will go to the [[Spirit Shores\|Spirit Shores]] when doing so. If they cannot access the Spirit Shores, they instead see darkness. When their bodies are asleep, dragons are not aware of their surroundings.
Due to their highly Invested nature, dragons would struggle to use most [[Invested Art\|Invested Arts]] or form bonds similar to the [[Nahel bond\|Nahel bond]]. In human form, dragons are able to manifest their soul, displaying a transparent and glowing image of their draconic form. They can release concentrated attacks of Investiture. Dragons are able to heal themselves with Investiture and are impervious to most modern firearms or poison. Even in human form, dragons can drop from any height and survive. One of the few ways a dragon can be killed is by a [[Type 1-6 Threnodite entity\|Type 1-6 Threnodite entity]].
When dragons die, their [[Cognitive shadow\|cognitive shadow]] stays in the Spirit Shores for their essence to fade away into the [[Beyond\|Beyond]], which can take millennia due to their highly invested nature. Similar to [[Shard\|Shards]], contracts are more binding to dragons.

### Connection
Dragons are Connected to each other, as well as their followers with a [[Tamukek\|tamukek]]. Using a tamukek, one may speak to a dragon, and the dragon can, in turn, respond and influence the speaker's emotions and pain in some manner similar to a [[Brass\|Soothing]] or [[Zinc\|Rioting]]. Dragons can temporarily take on prayers of another dragon.
Dragons can also feel and manipulate the emotions of those around them.

## Culture
>“*The deal has been struck. I must pay you, however little I think the trade was worth.*”
\-Xisisrefliel to Tress.[35]


Most dragons are fond of bargains; in some cases, this can take the form of magical interactions that involve the dragon granting the petitioner a boon. They are likewise fond of collecting useful assets, which includes people, in form of slaves, followers or even corporate employees. They like to make [[#Cultural Influences]] throughout the cosmere.
At some point, dragons agreed to officially step down from direct political roles, believing they are causing stagnancy or strife. Aside from that, draconic personalities vary. [[Frost\|Frost]] follows a strict non-interventionist policy; some take their self-appointed duties towards people extremely seriously; others devote themselves to scholarship; yet others simply do not care and do as they wish. Many dragons do not keep to the ideal of refraining from direct political involvement. For example, [[Xisisrefliel\|Xisisrefliel]] follows it more loosely, leading a company with significant influence in the cosmere.
Most dragons have at least two names -- a full, long name often used for religious purposes and a shorter nickname for ease of interaction (for example, Xisisrefliel corresponds with some people using the name "Foil").
It is considered dangerous to disturb a sleeping dragon. There are also myths about dragons having gigantic hoards of gold and of eating men.

### Technology
The dragons have had highly advanced technology prior to the modernization of other planets. They were able to build magnificent cities such as [[Lar'Cal\|Lar'Cal]] or the [[Silverlight\|Silverlight]] palaces. They could also fabricate locks widely believed to be impenetrable.
However, Yolen has fallen behind technologically compared to many other planets in the cosmere. This is due to certain things that they can access and [[Shard\|Shardic]] influence.

## Lesser Dragons
Aside from the so-called "greater dragons," the cosmere is also home to lesser dragons. Lesser dragons have some notable differences to greater dragons; they do not share the amphibian-like lifecycle of greater dragons, and are incapable of transformation into humanoid form. An unnamed planet is home to a variety of non-hexapedal lesser dragons known as **drakelings**. Lesser dragons have a much easier time using Invested Arts.
The cosmere is also home to dragons that are not immortal and cannot shape-shift. These dragons have human-level intelligence and have been known to let humans ride on them. It is unclear if these dragons are the same species as the previously mentioned lesser dragons or drakelings. Nothing else is known about the biology or abilities of lesser dragons, and their relationship (if any) to greater dragons is unclear.

### Yolen
Even prior to the [[Shattering\|Shattering]], dragons were much more cosmere-aware than humans and [[Sho Del\|Sho Del]], and a few may have gone off-world before anyone else did. On [[Yolen\|Yolen]], dragons were worshipped as deities and considered themselves divine in turn, with believers praying to them through tamukeks. Dragons who rejected their position as deities, such as [[Koravellium Avast\|Koravellium Avast]], were seen as heretics. [[Adonalsium\|Adonalsium]], however, was not a dragon.
Three dragons are known to have been involved in the Shattering of Adonalsium, with one being Koravellium Avast, who would go on to take up the [[Shard\|Shard]] of [[Cultivation\|Cultivation]], and Medelantorius, who took up the Shard of [[Valor\|Valor]]. Another dragon, [[Frost\|Frost]], would remain on Yolen after the Shattering, assuming a stance of non-intervention, although he would amass a significant knowledge of the wider cosmere through his relations with both the [[Seventeenth Shard\|Seventeenth Shard]] and [[Hoid\|Hoid]].
Dragons possess a sort of "meditative realm" that contains the "wisdom of the ancient dead." This realm can be accessed via a tamukek. Further details about this realm are currently unclear.

### Direct Influences
Dragons are said to live to inspire others, although many don’t live up to this ideal. The best of them do however, spending their entire existences sending comfort, confidence, and compassion to those who prayed to them.
The [[Cognitive Realm\|Cognitive Realm]]-located city of [[Silverlight\|Silverlight]] was originally built as a collection of dragon palaces. Even in the present day, as the city grew to house worldhoppers of other species, dragons retain a substantial presence in the city, owning corporations, vast establishments and even entire skyscrapers. However, even dragons who would consider Silverlight their home base occassionally depart for a long time. One such dragon is [[Xisisrefliel\|Xisisrefliel]], who for centuries has been living underneath the [[Crimson Sea\|Crimson Sea]] on [[Lumar\|Lumar]] in order to research the breakdown of aethers and aether spores.
When dragons do settle, they will often grant boons to the locals. Xisis receives gifts of human slaves in exchange for various boons both material, such as advanced off-world technology, and more insubstantial, like information or medical treatment. Over on [[Roshar\|Roshar]], Koravellium Avast grants her own boons to various petitioners, though rather than taking anything in exchange, she grants a matching curse. Whether this is her own choice or a compulsion is unclear.

### Cultural Influences
>“*Like a grandmother tucking away that bit of string that wrapped her package, the dragons want to know they have a certain number of easily influenced cultures around, for the proverbial rainy day.*”
\-Hoid[15]


Dragons frequently reveal themselves to developing human cultures, so as to plant the seeds for later use; the term "dragon" is known to nigh-on every cosmere culture. The effectiveness of this approach varies, however. On [[Roshar\|Roshar]], despite the world being home to a draconic Shard, dragons are virtually unknown in the present times, although ancient artwork depicting draconic creatures suggests this was not always the case. On [[Nalthis\|Nalthis]], on the other hand, dragons are well-known mythological creatures, with the phrase "dragon's nest" referring to someplace highly dangerous. Similarly, dragons are a familiar concept on [[Lumar\|Lumar]], though it's unclear how well-known they were before Xisis moved there.
The planet of [[Komashi\|Komashi]] appears to be particularly taken with dragons. They were popular in the times of the [[Torio\|Torio]] kingdom, with sculptures depicting them frequently used as decorations. Centuries later, in [[Nagadan\|Nagadan]], dragons remained a popular motif, with plush dragons being sold as childrens' toys, and one of the years of the Nagadan calendar being named the year of the dragon. Despite this, Komashi myths seem to paint at least some dragons as antagonistic, as they have stories of dragon-slaying heroes.
In many cultures, dragons are thought to hoard material wealth, which [[Hoid\|Hoid]] theorizes is due to the ridges of dragonsteel that cover their bodies. Many people also believe that dragons eat humans. It appears to be common for people to think that all dragons are royalty, although this isn’t true.

## Trivia
Though still present in the cosmere in background roles, dragons are not slated to become major players until the [[Dragonsteel (series)\|Dragonsteel series]].
Brandon likes dragons a lot; they are also the only classical fantasy race to feature in the [[Cosmere\|cosmere]]. However, the early books in the cosmere were published at the time where "dragon books", such as ** and ** were extremely popular. As such, Brandon felt that the market was "dragon flooded" and postponed involving dragons in his stories until much later.
[[Hoid\|Hoid]] has dated a dragon at some point.
