## Appearance and Personality
Rebeke is a woman in her twenties-- while [[Nomad\|Nomad]] initially estimates her age as being in her early twenties, she claims she is about a decade or so younger than Nomad, who was thirty-eight [[Rosharan\|Rosharan]] years old when he stopped aging, but he looks younger.
She has black hair, which she wears in a braid, that has silver in it.
She has light green eyes and pale skin, features she shares with [[Elegy\|Elegy]].
Rebeke has a [[Cinderheart\|cinderheart]], but unlike other Charred, Rebeke's sits embedded at the surface of the skin, leaving her chest structurally intact, though with areas of ashen skin around it.


## Attributes and Abilities
Rebeke is a somewhat skilled pilot, able to pilot the [[Dawnchaser\|Dawnchaser]] through the great maelstrom beyond sunset with just the instruments.
Due to her cinderheart being siphoned off before fully implanting, she is in possession of a set of abilities unlike other Charred. She also does not have the cognitive effects of the cinderheart, meaning that she does not feel the rage, and maintains her memories. Like the [[Cinder King\|Cinder King]], Rebeke has the ability to control still corrupted Charred through their cinderhearts, though if there is another will pushing against hers, her control is limited. It is unclear whether she has enhanced strength like other Charred, though the Cinder King does have enhanced strength from being [[Investiture\|Invested]], it is possible that Rebeke does as well.

## History
Shortly before the arrival of Nomad, Rebeke embarked on a mission along with [[Divinity\|Divinity]] to rescue Elegy. Although Divinity died in the attempt, she was able to rescue Elegy and [[Thomos\|Thomos]], with the help of Nomad. After returning to Beacon, she is assigned to be Nomad's babysitter by the [[Greater Good\|Greater Good]].. In her babysitting duty, she and Nomad, run across a scout from [[Union\|Union]], whom they run down, though not before the scout got a call out to the Cinder King's forces. Despite his promise of no trickery, the Cinder King takes Rebeke hostage in an attempt to manipulate Nomad. After being freed, more of the Cinder Kings forces attack Beacon, during which Rebeke pilots the ship that Nomad is on and helps him rescue the Greater Good.
