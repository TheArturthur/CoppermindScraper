**Talenel**, known as  **Talenelat'Elin** in [[Vorinism\|Vorinism]] and **Tuk** in [[Tukar\|Tukar]] and nicknamed **Taln** or **Talat**, is one of the ten [[Herald\|Heralds]] of the [[Almighty\|Almighty]] and patron of the [[Order of Stonewards\|Order of Stonewards]]. He is often titled the **Herald of War**, or the **Herald of Soldiers**. At [[Aharietiam\|Aharietiam]], he was abandoned by the rest of the Heralds and forced to stay in [[Damnation\|Damnation]] alone. He returned shortly before the arrival of the [[Everstorm\|Everstorm]], his mind ruined by torture, to bring warning of the upcoming [[True Desolation\|True Desolation]].

## Appearance and Personality
  Taln as patron of the Stonewards
### Appearance
For more images, see [[Talenel/Gallery\|/Gallery]].
Taln is a tall, dark-haired, stoutly built man, with thick arms like a soldier or laborer. He is dark-skinned, appearing almost [[Makabaki\|Makabaki]] in coloration and his eyes are a dark brown color as well. Taln also wears a beard. He is well-muscled, and his hands are covered in calluses. After his reappearance on [[Roshar\|Roshar]] in the [[Era of Solitude\|Era of Solitude]] he is disheveled looking, with broken nails, long, matted, and ragged hair, as well as bits of wood and leaves tangled in his beard. His clothing at his return is merely a ragged piece of cloth wrapped around his waist. Taln's eyes appear wild and mad and he stands with a straight back and his head upright when he is aware of his surroundings. When Taln is in his stupor, his eyes are dull and expressionless and he sits, slumped and morose. Taln speaks perfect Alethi with a northern accent.

### Personality
Taln was known to be soft-spoken and caring prior to [[Aharietiam\|Aharietiam]]. Of the Heralds, he held the least regal position prior to the founding of the [[Oathpact\|Oathpact]]. Something drove him to attempt to kill [[Cultivation\|Cultivation]]; and he held animosity towards [[Honor\|Honor]], blaming him for the destruction on [[Ashyn\|Ashyn]]. Furthermore, he was willing to take on the role of [[Herald\|Herald]], while knowing the suffering he would experience, so that nobody else would have to suffer in his place.
As patron of the [[Stonewards\|Stonewards]], Taln was a man who exemplified the traits they found valuable, including resolve, strength, and dependability. He was also self-sacrificing, often being killed during [[Desolation\|Desolations]] in order to achieve victories thought to be impossible. When men fight and die, nothing could prevent Taln from fighting alongside them. This penchant for stubbornness as well as his selflessness are likely what allowed Taln to resist the torture in [[Damnation\|Damnation]] for over four thousand years to prevent a Desolation from coming to Roshar. Upon his realization that he had been betrayed by the other Heralds, Taln felt only joy that mankind had been given enormous time to recover and might finally have a chance at defeating [[Odium\|Odium]] in the [[True Desolation\|True Desolation]].
After the torture in Damnation, Taln's mind was heavily damaged. Upon his return to [[Roshar\|Roshar]], Taln displayed true awareness only at his arrival in [[Kholinar\|Kholinar]] and a handful of other times. [[Dalinar\|Dalinar]] opening [[Honor's Perpendicularity\|Honor's Perpendicularity]] allowed him to temporarily become fully lucid. Most of the time Taln spends dully repeating a mantra about the coming Desolations and the preparations that mankind must make to fight the [[Voidbringers\|Voidbringers]]. Sometimes he speaks at a whisper, sometimes at a shout, but always he stares unseeingly. His thoughts are confused and scattered, at times nonsensical. He often thinks of his time being tortured, and seems to perceive the world around him as being on fire. He has some dim recognition that he has returned too late to stop the coming Desolation. Taln's battle reflexes seem sharp still, and he may still react to attacks, such as when he saved [[Amaram\|Amaram's]] life by snatching a dart out of the air, although fighting itself terrifies him. Taln may also react to [[Surgebinding\|Surgebinding]] done in his vicinity with shock and aggression, although he is able to recognize the person Surgebinding as a [[Knights Radiant\|Radiant]]. Similarly, the arrival of a [[Peakspren\|Peakspren]] has been known to give Taln a few lucid moments. However, in these instances, Taln soon lapses back into his waking non-responsiveness and is unable to be roused again. He is capable of some limited movement in this state, such as lying down, but spends most of his time merely sitting. His trauma has caused many to dismiss him as a simple madman.

## Attributes and Abilities
 
### Herald of War
For more information about general Heraldic powers, see [[/wiki/Herald#Abilities]].
As one of the [[Herald\|Heralds]] of the [[Almighty\|Almighty]], Taln is a [[Cognitive Shadow\|Cognitive Shadow]] capable of resurrection. Upon death, he returns to [[Damnation\|Damnation]] to be tortured unceasingly, and returns to [[Roshar\|Roshar]] when he or any other Herald breaks. He is ageless, like the other Heralds. He is the only Herald who has never broken.
Serving as the Herald of War, Taln was one of the most skilled fighters on [[Roshar\|Roshar]]. He was the most formidable fighter among the Heralds without dispute. His reflexes are inhumanly quick, and he is even stronger than his massive build would suggest, able to throw open the gates of Kholinar on his own. He is capable of moving at incredible speeds, and can move so fast that the air displacement of his passing can shatter windows. He is an incredible fighter even without a weapon, and is capable of taking on dozens of Fused with his bare hands. He was once a capable commander, able to win battles thought to be unwinnable, even at the cost of his own life. He also excelled at training soldiers, and helped prepare mankind's warriors for the Desolations.

### Shardwielding and Surgebinding
As a Herald, Talenel has access to his [[Honorblade\|Honorblade]], an Invested weapon given to him by the [[Shard\|Shard]] [[Honor\|Honor]]. Like all [[Shardblade\|Shardblades]], it's able to cut through any material effortlessly. This increased his already great potency on the battlefield, particularly against [[Thunderclast\|thunderclasts]], as his Blade can cut their stone bodies easily. Having spent centuries using the Honorblade in the fight against the [[Voidbringers\|Voidbringers]], Taln was highly experienced in its use. The Stormfather considers him to be indisputably the greatest duelist among all the Heralds.
The Honorblade granted Taln access to the [[Surge\|Surges]] of [[/wiki/Surgebinding#Cohesion]] and [[/wiki/Surgebinding#Tension]]. Unlike the [[Knights Radiant\|Knights Radiant]], he had no need for [[Stormlight\|Stormlight]], as his Surgebinding was powered directly by Honor. It's unknown if this has been impacted by Honor's death. What his Surges did is unclear at this point. In his prime, there was likely nobody in the [[Cosmere\|cosmere]] who could defeat him in one-on-one combat.
Like the other Heralds, he would have, over time, developed the ability to draw upon the power of Roshar itself, not simply the Surges of Honor. Around four thousand five hundred fifty years before the Night of Sorrows, Honor granted him, and the other Heralds, greater access to the power of the Surges and to the powers of Roshar.
Taln's Honorblade was swapped. The weapon he arrived with in [[Kholinar\|Kholinar]] was shaped like an enormous spike, long, narrow, and straight. The weapon he arrived with on the Shattered Plains was wide and cleaver-like, and screams when touched by a Radiant, marking it as a dead Shardblade. The Stormfather confirms the Shardblade Taln had when he reached the Shattered Plains was not an Honorblade, but does not know what happened to it.. At some point on his journey from Kholinar to the Shattered Plains,  and the  swapped his Blade.

## History
  Being tortured on <<Braize\|Braize>>
### Early Life
Talenel was born on Roshar over seven-thousand years before the True Desolation, and was a soldier and bodyguard. His grandmother was born on [[Ashyn\|Ashyn]], and he blamed [[Honor\|Honor]] for its destruction. Sometime before joining the Oathpact, Taln attempted to kill [[Cultivation\|Cultivation]], but did not succeed. As a result of his attempt, his soul became warped. At some point, Taln also received a weapon from [[Kalak\|Kalak]], but lost it. Later on, Taln was dismissed from the front lines and forbidden from having a weapon, and became a horse keeper.

>“*I will protect the people of this land, I will hold back the darkness. Not for Honor. But I’ll do it.*”
\-Taln joining the Oathpact[13]

Taln was not initially intended to be among the Heralds, but Nale suggested him as the tenth Herald after [[Hoid\|Hoid]] refused to become one. Though [[Tanavast\|Tanavast]] didn’t like the idea, Nale argued that Taln had interacted with the gods in the past, as Tanavast had wanted, and that Taln would represent the common people that they would protect. Nale wanted someone who would understand the struggles of the common man, and who would provide contrary opinions, as he was the only Herald who was not a king, scholar, or general. The others who would become Heralds, along with Hoid and Honor, approached Taln to ask him to join the Oathpact. Taln initially rejected Honor’s offer, but then asked what the price would be. Once Taln learned of the suffering he’d also be taking on, and that they would choose someone else if he refused, he accepted their offer and joined the Oathpact as a Herald. Taln swore to protect the people, but he did not swear his oath to Honor like the other Heralds.

### Herald of the Almighty
As Herald, he and the others fought in a series of Desolations against the [[Voidbringer\|Voidbringers]] and [[Odium\|Odium]] in defense of mankind. During each Desolation, Taln trained soldiers to fight the Voidbringers. After each Desolation, Taln and the others returned to [[Braize\|Braize]], where they were tortured. Each time, if Taln himself was killed during the fighting, he automatically returned to Braize. After several Desolations, the [[Knights Radiant\|Knights Radiant]] were founded by [[Surgebinding\|Surgebinders]] with [[Ishar\|Ishar's]] help and Taln became patron of the [[Order of Stonewards\|Order of Stonewards]], who shared access to the same Surges he possessed. Throughout all the Desolations, Taln never once broke under torture during his time on Braize.

### Aharietiam
After around three-thousand years of Desolations, during [[Aharietiam\|Aharietiam]], also known as the Last Desolation, Talenel chose to attempt to hold a passage by a northern waterway, a near impossible task. While he managed to defend it successfully, he was killed in the fighting and returned to Damnation. However, Taln was the only Herald to die in this Desolation. All of the other surviving Heralds, wearied by millennia of torture, all agreed that they did not want to return to Braize. Honor informed them that as long as one Herald remained bound to the Oathpact, it could end the cycle of Desolations, as one was a number of power in the Cosmere. The other Heralds agreed to abandon Taln to suffer in Braize alone and become the sole bearer of the Oathpact. They abandoned their Honorblades, and Taln alone remained bound to the Oathpact and returned to Damnation to be tortured.

### Return to Kholinar
  Taln arriving in <<Kholinar\|Kholinar>>
>“*Who am I? I . . . I am Talenel’Elin, Stonesinew, Herald of the Almighty. The [[True Desolation\|Desolation]] has come. Oh, God . . . it has come. And I have failed.*”
\-Talenel[7]


Around Tanat 1167, [[Chanarach\|Chanarach]] died and returned to Braize. Around the Tanat 1174 of the Vorin Calendar, not long after the [[Battle of the Tower\|Battle of the Tower]], Chanarach broke and she and Taln both returned to Roshar from Damnation. Taln was covered in condensation and had twigs and leaves stuck in his beard as he quickly rushed to [[Kholinar\|Kholinar]], cutting through the city gate with his [[Honorblade\|Honorblade]] to give warning to the [[Alethi\|Alethi]] that the [[True Desolation\|True Desolation]] was upon them. [[Hoid\|Hoid]] knew when and where Taln would arrive and was waiting in Kholinar for him. After his proclamation, Taln promptly collapsed.

### Travel to the Shattered Plains
After his arrival in Kholinar, Taln was apprehended by the men of [[House Kholin\|House Kholin]]. Taln displayed no awareness following his collapse, repeating his mantra with no regard for his surroundings. Due to the odd circumstances of his arrival and his possession of a Shardblade, Taln was brought to the Shattered Plains by Bordin, one of Dalinar Kholin's most trusted servants. Hoid accompanied them for a portion of the journey, but along the way ran off into the hills. At some point between Taln's arrival in Kholinar and his arrival at the Shattered Plains,  and the , swapped Taln's Honorblade with a regular Shardblade. Bordin brought Taln to Dalinar and Elhokar Kholin, who tried to question him on his past and the origin of his Blade, but they determined nothing. Dalinar and Elhokar did not put any thought into his claims that he was a Herald, believing him a simple madman. Dalinar sent Taln to a monastery in his warcamps in the care of his ardents, hoping they could do something to alleviate his madness. Dalinar also took Taln's Shardblade, bonding it himself as a part of his investigation into Kaladin's claims that Amaram was a murderer and a thief.

>“*One of Ishar’s Knights. I remember… He founded them? Yes. Several Desolations ago. No longer just talk. It hasn’t been talk for thousands of years. but… When…*”
\-Taln after seeing Shallan take in Stormlight.[10]

The ardents placed Taln in a small, dimly lit cell in their monastery where he remained unresponsive despite their care. Once Taln was placed in the monastery, several groups became aware of his presence on the Shattered Plains. The [[Ghostbloods\|Ghostbloods]] became aware of him somehow and were interested in him because he had drawn the attention of the king and Dalinar. Their source provided them with sketches of Taln. The Sons of Honor became aware when Dalinar ordered Bordin go drinking with some Amaram's men and talk about Taln. As part of his investigation into Amaram, Dalinar wished Bordin to spread the lie that Taln had mentioned a cache of Shardblades on the Plains. This news spread to Amaram, who contacted Bordin and had him bring him to see Taln in the monastery.  At this same time, Shallan Davar visited Taln on the behalf of the Ghostbloods. Shallan recorded his mantra but was unable to get a reaction out of him until she drew in some [[Stormlight\|Stormlight]], provoking a sudden and violent reaction from Taln, who shoved her into a wall. Appearing semi-lucid, Taln identified her as a Radiant, but quickly lapsed back into his mantra shortly afterwards. Shallan decided that she believed him to be a simple madman. While Shallan was still in the room with Taln, Amaram and Bordin arrived to see Taln, so she concealed herself in the cell with Lightweaving. Upon seeing Taln, Amaram determined that he believed the man to be a Herald, but was unable to get him to speak or interact at all. Wishing to recover the fictitious Blades, Amaram left Taln in the cell.

### Held By Amaram
 
A few weeks later, after the [[Battle of Narak\|Battle of Narak]], Amaram broke into the monastery, cutting through the wall of Taln's cell with his Shardblade. While Amaram tried to get Taln on his feet and into the carriage, Iyatil attempted to assassinate Amaram with a blowgun. Taln reflexively caught both darts with his hands, and Iyatil fled. Taln seemed to be conscious for a moment, examining the dart with interest, but then he returned to his stupor. Amaram was unable to rouse him and so he put him into his carriage, heading for [[Urithiru\|Urithiru]].
Amaram smuggled Taln through the Oathgate at Narak and to Urithiru, and afterwards he brought him with him to [[Thaylen City\|Thaylen City]] where Amaram was supervising the reconstruction of the city following the damage of the [[Everstorm\|Everstorm]]. Taln was kept in a tent built against the city wall under the guard of Sadeas soldiers. During this time, the Ghostbloods learned of Taln's location, information Mraize shared with the Herald [[Shalash\|Shalash]].

### The Battle of Thaylen Field
>“*Four thousand years? Ash… Ash. What a wonderful thing.*”
\-Taln to Shalash[6]


During the [[Battle of Thaylen Field\|Battle of Thaylen Field]], Ash snuck into Taln's tent, which was left unguarded when Sadeas's soldiers were corrupted by Odium. She attempted to rouse Taln with no success. When Dalinar Ascended, swearing the Third Ideal of the Bondsmiths and summoning [[Honor's Perpendicularity\|Honor's Perpendicularity]], this seemed to awaken Taln. Ash explained that the Heralds had abandoned them, but he expressed only joy at the opportunity their betrayal had given mankind. He felt Dalinar waiting for them, although he did not know who was calling to him. He and Ash walked onto the battlefield, where they were recognized as Heralds by the Stormfather and Dalinar. Shortly thereafter, Taln's lucidity faded again, and he hid cowering from the fighting. After the battle was over, Ash tried to get Taln through the Oathgate, hoping to bring him to Ishar, who she believed could help him. However as they waited in line, they felt the true death of Jezrien and Taln began to whimper and collapse. As they inadvertently caused a commotion, they were recognized by [[Jasnah Kholin\|Jasnah Kholin]], who compared their faces to drawings she had received from Hoid. Taln and Ash both accompanied Dalinar to the [[Emuli offensive\|Emuli offensive]], so that Jasnah could try and get historical information out of them.

### Battle of Azimir
  Taln's final stand
>“*And for the first time in over four thousand years, the Bearer of Agonies *fought back*.*”
\-Ash watching Taln attack the Fused[11]


After returning from Emul, Taln and Ash were kept in an Azish hospital in Azimir, near the Oathgate. During the [[Battle of Azimir\|Battle of Azimir]], [[Abidi\|Abidi the Monarch]] entered into the room where Ash and Taln were sitting. He ordered his Fused to kill them, and Taln stopped muttering his usual mantra. Taln stood up, causing Abidi to immediately flee. Taln proceeded to launch into battle, killing dozens of Fused with his bare hands by ripping apart their chests and crushing their gemhearts. Ash joined him in battle, wielding a sword. They were both killed in the fight, but managed to kill almost every Fused in the city, of eight different brands. When Adolin found his body upon a pile of Fused corpses, Taln had been pierced by a dozen lances, and still held the crushed skull of a Fused in his hand.
With the formation of the new Oathpact by Kaladin and Ishar, Taln’s mind joined Kaladin and the other Heralds on a restored version of Ashyn, before it was destroyed. There, the Fused could not reach him, thus giving him an opportunity to recover. While Taln told Kaladin that he forgave the other Heralds for their betrayal, he still did not want to talk to them immediately, needing time to recover.

## Culture
As one of the Heralds, Taln has impacted mythology and culture in a variety of ways across all of Roshar. In the [[Vorin\|Vorin]] nations, he is known as **Talenelat'Elin** and **Stonesinew**. There, he is revered as a warrior who helped to drive the Voidbringers from Roshar and has now taken the fight to the [[Tranquiline Halls\|Tranquiline Halls]] with the other Heralds. Like all Heralds, he has temples dedicated to himself in major cities like [[Thaylen City\|Thaylen]] and [[Kholinar\|Kholinar]]. Temple art of him sometimes depicts him facing off against hundreds of voidbringers, fighting alone. The [[Order of Talenelat\|Order of Talenelat]] is a lay devotary based in Alethkar, dedicated to him. Taln is also the namesake of a major Rosharan constellation, [[Taln's Scar\|Taln's Scar]]. Traditionally, he's associated with the number 9 (tanat) the [[Ten Essences\|Essence]] of Talus, and the Divine Attributes of Dependable and Resourceful.
Some people refer to Taln as the **Bearer of Agonies**, and swear by his name, while the [[Sons of Honor\|Sons of Honor]] call him the **Great Prince**. "Taln's palms" and "Taln's nails" are both rarely-used exclamations. "Talat's hand" appears to be a related one.
He is referred to as the **Ancient of Stones** in the [[Diagram (literature)\|Diagram]]. It is unclear if this title is unique to the Diagram, or if it is in more general use amongst Vorins.
While his role in the western culture isn't well-known, he is counted among the lesser gods under [[Tashi\|Tashi]]. In [[Tashikk\|Tashikk]], one of the traditional ten pancakes eaten during the [[Weeping\|Weeping]] are dedicated to him.

## Taln's Mantra
 
Below is the full text of Taln's mantra that he repeats when he is in his stupor. There is some variation to it, but this is the typical form it takes.

>“*I am Talenel'Elin, Herald of War. The time of the Return, the [[Desolation\|Desolation]], is near at hand. We must prepare. You will have forgotten much, following the destruction of the times past. [[Kalak\|Kalak]] will teach you to cast bronze, if you have forgotten this. We will [[Soulcast\|Soulcast]] blocks of metal directly for you. I wish we could teach you steel, but casting is so much easier than forging, and you must have something we can produce quickly. Your stone tools will not serve against what is to come. [[Vedel\|Vedel]] can train your surgeons, and [[Jezrien\|Jezrien]] . . . he will teach you leadership. So much is lost between Returns . . . I will train your soldiers. We should have time. [[Ishar\|Ishar]] keeps talking about a way to keep information from being lost following Desolations. And you have discovered something unexpected. We will use that. Surgebinders to act as guardians . . . Knights . . . The coming days will be difficult, but with training, humanity will survive. You must bring me to your leaders. The other [[Heralds\|Heralds]] should join us soon.*”
\-Talenel'Elin[12]
