## Geography
Alm is a small nation bordered by [[Yezier\|Yezier]] and [[Liafor\|Liafor]]. It is also adjacent to [[Shinovar\|Shinovar]], although the two nations are separated by the [[Misted Mountains\|Misted Mountains]]. Most of Alm's territory lies between a large river and the mountains. It also has a small coastline on a bay connected to the [[Aimian Sea\|Aimian Sea]]. The land is fertile enough for crops, and the large river in southeastern Alm provides additional sustenance. There are also a type of fruit that only grows in Alm, this fruit is often dried before it’s eaten, and sometimes made into wine.
Alm has an area around 144,000 km.

## Culture
Since Alm's population is ethnically Makabaki, its people generally have dark skin and hair, and the local language is presumably a part of the Azish language family. Alm and the nearby nation of [[Desh\|Desh]] are considered to be more rural than other Azish nations, and local fashion consists of clothing that is muted and lighter than that of their neighbors. Its population is incredibly small, and it doesn’t produce any noteworthy exports.
The people of Alm pride themselves on being self-sustained. Alm has no major cities, and its designated diplomat family lives in a quaint palace near the shared border with Yezier. Citizens live in small villages or on remote homesteads, farming the nearby land. They are generally disinterested in competition and vanity. When someone experiences a major life event such as coming of age, marriage, or the death of a loved one, it’s customary to travel to the river in the southeast to feel the transitory nature of life, then travel to the mountains in the west to feel a sense of permanence.

## Politics
As a member of the Azish Empire, Alm is self-governing, but recognizes the [[Prime Aqasix\|Prime Aqasix]] as their emperor and generally follows [[Azir\|Azir]]’s lead in international politics. Alm remains an enigma to most of Roshar. To maintain good relations with Azir and its Bronze Palace, Alm sends bundles of dried fruits and ambassadors. In turn, Azir has often sent enslaved [[Singer\|singers]], although, unbeknownst to Azir, these are freed immediately upon their arrival in Alm. During the True Desolation, Alm secretly became a hub for singers who wish to support neither side of the global conflict. Despite this, the people from Alm strive to maintain any protection it is entitled to as part of the empire.

## History
### Heraldic Epochs
During the [[Silver Kingdoms\|Silver Kingdoms]] era, Alm was part of [[Makabakam\|Makabakam]].

### Era of Solitude
During the [[Era of Solitude\|Era of Solitude]], it joined other Makabaki nations to form the Azish Empire.
At some point after the [[Recreance\|Recreance]] and the subsequent [[Scouring of Aimia\|scouring of Aimia]], Alm experienced an influx of refugees from Aimia, although it is not certain when exactly this occurred and at what rate. It is also unclear how many refugees immigrated to Alm and what impact this might have had on the local culture and population.
[[Lift\|Lift]] once visited Alm and enjoyed her time there, since relatively few people attempted to murder her.

### The True Desolation
Due to their location on the western edge of Roshar, [[Steen\|Steen]] and Alm were the first nations to report the arrival of the [[Everstorm\|Everstorm]], which worried [[Ghenna\|Ghenna]] and the government of [[Tashikk\|Tashikk]].
Both Alm and Desh had ambassadors in [[Azimir\|Azimir]] when [[Yanagawn\|Yanagawn]] agreed to visit [[Urithiru\|Urithiru]]. After a while, the nation secretly became a hub for singers who wish to support neither side of the global conflict.
